Fundraising Mission: Get our Brothers to WDC!

$0 of $750

Western District Conference gives our brothers a chance to be part of something bigger than our chapter, making a difference across the West Coast by attending leadership workshops, learning from other chapters, and collaborating with other outstanding musicians.
Getting there, however, is an expensive commitment, as this year it's being hosted in Portland, OR, a 13 hour drive for our San Luis Obispo chapter. Gas for the trip, hotel costs, registration, and meals have made the conference less accessible for our members. That's why we're asking for your help.
In the spirit of brotherhood and whimsy, we're launching the WDC Flat Stanley fundraiser. You choose what you'd like to sponsor, and we'll bring a personalized flat Stanley of the donor with us on our trip, taking photos along the way with them to show off what we're able to do- because of you!
Flat Stanley is a popular educational activity in K-12 education where a kid draws themself on a 2-D body and mails it to a relative, celebrity, or friend somewhere interesting. The receiver takes photos with their flat Stanley, makes a little journal, and sends it back, possibly with a few souvenirs. We're taking this spirit of adventure with us, so that as a community, we can make it to WDC.
Learn more about the Flat Stanley project at www.flatstanleyproject.com.

Fundraising Breakdown

Registration Fee

The registration fee for this season has not yet been released, but is predicted to cost between $100-$130 per person in attendance.

Hotel

We plan to stay for two nights, sharing one room with about four members. Each room will cost around $220 per room per night.

Meals

Members are on their own for all meals outside of the WDC banquet dinner. Breakfast's will be included with the hotel, leaving members to cover lunch, dinner, and any road snacks for the trip, ~$125 per person.

Gas

At an average 20 miles per gallon, and 850 miles each way, every car we send will spend about $400 on gas assuming an average of $5/gallon along the route.
